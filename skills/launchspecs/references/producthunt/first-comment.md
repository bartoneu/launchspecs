# Product Hunt — First comment guide

The maker's first comment is captured at submission time and posted automatically when the launch goes live. It appears right below the gallery on the launch page — the most highly visible piece of long-form writing on the entire post after the tagline.

## Why it matters (PH-cited stats)

- **70% of products that achieved Product of the Day, Week, or Month had a maker first comment.** ([Launch Guide](https://www.producthunt.com/launch/preparing-for-launch))
- The first comment is the only place where the maker can speak directly to the community at scale on launch day.
- It surfaces below the gallery — the second-most-read piece of writing after the tagline.

## CRITICAL: PH bans LLM-generated comments

> *"Product Hunt is about person-to-person interactions. Our community wants to talk to and hear from real people. **No LLMs or Chrome extensions please!**"* — [Commenting Guidelines](https://help.producthunt.com/en/articles/10030102-commenting-guidelines)

This rule applies to **all** comments, including the maker's first comment.

Comments that read as LLM-generated may be:
- Flagged for moderation review
- Auto-hidden
- Removed
- Lead to repeated-violation account restrictions

**Implication for using AI tools (including this skill) to prepare a first comment:** the AI can produce a *scaffold* with placeholder phrasing and structure, but the published comment **must be in the maker's own voice** — rewritten with personal anecdotes, specific phrasing, and details that only the maker would know.

## Required content elements (per Launch Guide)

A strong first comment includes:

1. **Personal hook** — your real connection to the problem. (Founder backstory, customer interview that triggered the build, something that frustrates you personally.)
2. **What it is** — 1-2 sentence product summary.
3. **Who it's for** — explicit target user. *"You can't be everything to everybody."*
4. **Key features** — short list (3-5 max), bold the feature names.
5. **What's different** — 1-2 sentences on what makes this not just another [category] tool.
6. **Pricing/offer note** — if you have a promo code, link/highlight it here.
7. **Ask for feedback (NOT upvotes)** — specific question(s) the maker wants the community to answer.
8. **Closing** — thank-you, an emoji optional.

## Format and tone (per PH staff and Commenting Guidelines)

- **Bullet points + bold formatting** improve scan-ability. PH provides formatting tools in the comment editor.
- **Keep it short.** Long comments get skipped.
- **Tone:** humble and helpful.
- **No marketing-speak.** *"Marketing-speak doesn't usually resonate very well."* (Launch Guide)
- **Plain language.** Specific over vague.
- **Emojis OK** but use sparingly and strategically (e.g. as bullet markers for a feature list, not for decoration).
- **Images, videos, GIFs are welcome** in the comment body — show, don't tell.

## What to avoid

From [Commenting Guidelines](https://help.producthunt.com/en/articles/10030102-commenting-guidelines):

- ❌ **Generic phrasing** — "congrats!", "looks great!", "thanks for building this!" — these don't add value and may be flagged as low-quality.
- ❌ **AI-generated text** — see above.
- ❌ **Asking for upvotes** — explicitly discouraged on PH.
- ❌ **Mass commenting style** — even your own first comment shouldn't read like a template.

## Profile completeness affects visibility

PH may auto-hide comments from accounts with sparse profiles. Before launch, ensure the maker's PH profile has:

- **Full real name** (first + last)
- **Clear profile photo** (no stock images, no placeholder, no logos)
- **Informative bio** reflecting professional/personal interests
- **Optional: connected verified socials** (X, LinkedIn) for added legitimacy

Sparse profiles → comments may be flagged for moderation review and hidden until reviewed.

## First comment scaffold template

Use this scaffold as a starting point. **Every section marked `[…]` must be rewritten in the maker's own voice before posting.**

```
Hey Product Hunt 👋

[PERSONAL HOOK — 1-2 sentences. The real story of why this product exists.
Examples of good hooks:
- "I've been a freelance designer for six years and every client kickoff still costs me half a day on intake forms, NDAs, and Figma access."
- "We were tired of starting every team meeting with 'wait, who has the latest version of the doc?'"
REWRITE THIS — make it specifically yours, not the templated version above.]

[PRODUCT_NAME] is [ONE-SENTENCE DESCRIPTION] for [SPECIFIC TARGET USER].

**What's in it:**
- **[Feature 1]** — [one-line benefit, not feature dump]
- **[Feature 2]** — [one-line benefit]
- **[Feature 3]** — [one-line benefit]

**What makes it different from [category]:**
[1-2 sentences. Be specific. Don't say "AI-powered" or "next-gen". Say what's actually different from the tool everyone is comparing you to.]

[OPTIONAL: PRICING/OFFER NOTE]
For the PH community, we're offering [SPECIFIC OFFER] — use code [CODE] (link in the launch page).

[FEEDBACK ASK — be specific]
Examples:
- "I'd love to hear from any [TARGET USER] in the community: does the [SPECIFIC WORKFLOW] match how you actually work?"
- "We're debating whether to add [FEATURE X] next — would that solve a problem for you?"
- "What would make this a no-brainer for your team?"

Thanks for taking a look 🙏
[YOUR NAME]
```

After producing this scaffold, the skill MUST output this notice prominently:

> ⚠️ **PH bans AI-generated comments.** This is a scaffold, not a turnkey comment. Before posting, rewrite every `[…]` placeholder in your own voice with real anecdotes and specific phrasing only you would use. Generic, templated, or LLM-sounding comments may be hidden by PH moderation.

## Pro idea from PH (Launch Guide)

Beyond the first comment, on launch day, **incorporate the product itself into your replies**. PH's own examples:

- The Loom team replied to community questions using Loom video links.
- The Figjam team set up a collaborative whiteboard for the community to play with.

This isn't required, but it's a strong move on launch day if your product type allows it.

## Operational notes

- Co-makers can comment on the launch — but only the *first* maker's comment is captured at submission. Other comments are posted manually after launch goes live.
- Don't write the first comment ON launch day under time pressure. Prep it during launchkit prep so launch day attention can go to engaging with replies.
- Have other team members comment shortly after launch goes live (not all at once — that triggers spam detection). Their comments should follow the same Commenting Guidelines.
