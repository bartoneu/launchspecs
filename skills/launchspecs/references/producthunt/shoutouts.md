# Product Hunt — Shoutouts guide

## What Shoutouts are

A way to acknowledge the tools and teams that helped bring your product to life — and a durable distribution lever.

Source: [Introducing Shoutouts](https://www.producthunt.com/stories/introducing-shoutouts) by Aaron O'Leary, PH staff, March 2024.

## Mechanics

- **Maximum: 3 shoutouts per launch.** Hard limit per [Launch Guide](https://www.producthunt.com/launch/preparing-for-launch).
- **Position in form:** appears as a sub-section in Step 3 (Makers), **after** you've selected your maker status. Per the announcement: *"After selecting yourself as a maker, you'll be presented with a new section called 'Shoutouts.'"*
- **Per shoutout:** select an existing PH product + add a "why" note explaining the contribution.
- **Notification:** when your launch goes live, PH automatically notifies the products you shouted out.

## What the shoutout becomes

Each shoutout shows up as a **founder review** on the recipient product's review page. Founder reviews:

- Sit **above** regular reviews (top of the reviews tab).
- Include a **link back to your profile** AND a **link to your product**.
- Persist on the recipient's page **long after launch day** — durable distribution surface.
- Get picked up in PH's LLM-driven discovery surfaces (per community discussion).

## Why this matters strategically

This is one of the simplest, highest-leverage distribution moves on PH. Three high-traffic shoutouts = three permanent founder-reviews on those products' pages, each linking back to your launch indefinitely.

The math:
- Your launch's homepage attention is 24 hours.
- A founder-review on Notion's PH page is permanent traffic, every time someone visits that page to read reviews.

## Selection strategy

PH's framing (from the announcement): *"Make sure you're spreading love to the ones who had the most impact on your journey."* Gratitude-first, distribution-second.

Pragmatic selection:

1. **Pick products you genuinely use** in building / operating your product. Specificity is what makes shoutouts useful as reviews.
2. **Bias toward higher-traffic PH products** when there are multiple genuine candidates — your shoutout sits on a more-trafficked surface.
3. **Spread the categories.** A design tool + a backend infra tool + a marketing tool covers more discovery surfaces than 3 design tools.
4. **Avoid generic mega-platforms** when a more specific tool also fits — "Slack" is a worse shoutout than the actual Slack-add-on you depend on, because the specificity makes the review useful.

## What the "why" note should say

The note becomes a public founder-review. It should read like a useful review, not a generic acknowledgment.

**Bad shoutout note:**
> "Thanks to Notion for being our knowledge base."

(Generic. Not useful to anyone reading Notion's reviews.)

**Good shoutout note:**
> "We use Notion's database views as the source of truth for our entire product roadmap and customer feedback. The synced databases between our public roadmap and our internal Linear pipeline cut our triage time from hours per week to minutes."

(Specific contribution + concrete benefit + reads like a useful product review.)

Each note should be **1-3 sentences**, name a specific feature/workflow, and describe a concrete benefit.

## Examples PH itself cites

From the [announcement](https://www.producthunt.com/stories/introducing-shoutouts), the categories of products you might shout out:

| Category | Example PH cited |
| --- | --- |
| Design tool | [Figma](https://www.producthunt.com/products/figma) |
| AI / model | [GPT-4](https://www.producthunt.com/products/gpt-4-2) |
| Marketing platform | [Beehiiv](https://www.producthunt.com/products/beehiiv) |
| Anything else meaningful | *"even the coffee machine fueling your late nights"* |

The breadth signals: anything that meaningfully contributed counts.

## LaunchKit prep checklist for Shoutouts

- [ ] Pick 3 candidate products well before launch day.
- [ ] Search PH (`producthunt.com/products/<slug>`) to confirm each one is on Product Hunt and has a product hub. (If a tool isn't on PH, you can't shout it out.)
- [ ] Pre-write the "why" note for each — keep it specific, 1-3 sentences, names a concrete feature/contribution.
- [ ] Verify the notes read like useful reviews, not generic acknowledgments — they will sit publicly on the recipient's page indefinitely.
- [ ] Decide who on the team picks the shoutouts (the maker who knows the actual tooling stack should drive this, not whoever happens to be doing the submission).
